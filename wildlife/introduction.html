Baundula national park

Explore the avian wonders of Bundala National Park, a birdwatcher's paradise in Sri Lanka! With its peak season from December to April, the park features captivating landscapes of lagoons, waterways, scrub jungles, and dunes. Home to 200 vibrant bird species, from charming little bee-eaters to extraordinary open-billed storks, Bundala promises exciting sightings. Don't miss the rare black-necked stork and the great thick-knee. Easy spottings include the blue-tailed bee-eater, spoonbill, red shank, green shank, and more. Witness the winter spectacle of greater flamingos from September to March. Embark on a birding adventure in Bundala National Park, where nature's beauty takes flight!

Gal Oya National Park

Embark on an off-the-beaten-path adventure in Gal Oya National Park, a tranquil haven in Sri Lanka's remote east. Enjoy a silent boat safari on the largest reservoir, picnicking on secluded islands amidst birdsong. Experience thrilling jeep safaris, catching sight of elephants and leopards. Established in 1954, this 63,000-hectare park showcases diverse wildlife, from axis deer to toque macaques and the elusive Sri Lankan leopard. With over 150 bird species, Ayurvedic plants, and a substantial elephant population, Gal Oya is a nature lover's paradise, offering a unique escape and enchanting biodiversity.

Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery

"Dive into the enchanting world of marine conservation with us! Globally, all seven species of marine turtles face endangerment, and Sri Lanka is a crucial nesting ground for five of these majestic creatures. Witness the awe-inspiring journey of marine turtles—from the nesting of eggs on our shores to the birth of adorable hatchlings and their courageous swim back to the sea. Our sanctuary, the Kosgoda Turtle Hatchery, not only operates as a hatchery but also serves as a haven for collected and rescued eggs. Join us in the incredible mission of hatching and releasing these precious lives back into the ocean. Your support makes a difference in preserving the wonders of marine life!"
